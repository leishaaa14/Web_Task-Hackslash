About UNESCO Indian Heritage Sites

UNESCO World Heritage Sites in India not only serve as repositories of religious history but also provide a glimpse into the architectural brilliance and cultural diversity that have shaped the country over centuries. Whether Buddhist, Hindu, or Jain, each site invites explorers to learn more about India's spiritual and historical roots. India accepted the convention on 14 November 1977, making its sites eligible for inclusion on the list.

There are 42 World Heritage Sites in India. Out of these, 34 are cultural, seven are natural, and one, Khangchendzonga National Park, is of mixed type. India has the sixth-most sites worldwide. The first sites to be listed were the Ajanta Caves, Ellora Caves, Agra Fort, and Taj Mahal, all of which were inscribed in the 1983 session of the World Heritage Committee. The most recent sites listed were Santiniketan and the Sacred Ensembles of the Hoysalas, in 2023.
